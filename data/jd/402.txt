Our Summer Internship Program is designed to provide meaningful, hands-on experience where you will work on real projects, solve real business challenges, and collaborate with leaders across the organization.
This is more than an internship—it’s an opportunity to accelerate your development, expand your perspective, and begin shaping your career with purpose.
We are looking for curious, driven, and high-potential individuals ready to learn, contribute, and grow. If you are eager to make an impact, challenge yourself, and be part of a team that values innovation and continuous improvement, we invite you to apply and take the first step toward your future with us.
About the Role
Dover Fueling Solutions is looking for a creative and motivated Summer Intern to join our team to enhance the quality and efficiency of EMV protocol testing efforts. This role offers an exciting opportunity to work on real-world fueling systems while gaining hands-on experience in software quality engineering.
Interns will have the opportunity to explore the domain, understand end-to-end system behavior, and contribute to building intuitive solutions that simplify complex technical workflows.
Key Responsibilities
Work with Product, Dev and QA to understand customer specific requirements
Create a library for customer specific test scripts to test all EMV protocol commands and events.
Create an automated solution to execute these scripts and report the results.
Preferred Qualifications
Basic understanding of testing concepts and knowledge of software development.
Ability to code in .net environment
Good understanding of xml and TCP/IP protocol
Basic understanding of Hardware and Embedded Software.
General IT knowledge.
Basic understanding of network administration.
A proactive attitude with a strong desire to learn.
What You’ll Gain
Hands-on experience in hardware setup, networking configurations, and test automation.
Exposure to fuel dispenser ecosystem and specifically the payment terminal
Mentorship from experienced QA and engineering professionals.
The opportunity to work with modern tools, frameworks, and engineering practices.
