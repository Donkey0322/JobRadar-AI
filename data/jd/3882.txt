About the Role:
The Calibration and Validation (CalVal) team supports all of Planet's satellite constellations by ensuring the geometric and radiometric accuracy of every pixel we deliver. The team ensures Planet's data products are accurate and interoperable through rigorous Calibration and Validation (CalVal) processes. The CalVal team works at scale and affects millions of images and terabytes of data. We are seeking a geometric engineering intern with a foundation in software development and linear algebra to contribute to the development and evaluation of sensor camera models and transformation algorithms at the core of our imagery pipeline.
This is a full-time, hybrid role which will require you to work from our San Francisco office 3 days per week.
Impact You'll Own:
Collaborate on the assessment and implementation of sensor camera models and spatial transformers
Designing and implementing automated scalable processes to apply to satellites to ensure that our products meet and exceed specifications and SLAs
Collaborate within a cross functional team to develop performance models, error budgets and calibration concepts
Troubleshooting geometric image quality issues and developing long-term mitigations and fixes
What You Bring:
Pursuing a degree in photogrammetry, engineering, physics, mathematics, or a related field relevant to the geometric characterization of space imaging platforms
Hands-on, collaborative mindset with effective interpersonal skills
Passion for programming, photography, remote sensing, Earth science, and geography
Experience with scientific computing in Python in a Unix/Linux environment
Experience with version control and CI/CD workflows
What Makes You Stand Out:
Strong foundation in linear algebra, coordinate transformations, and nonlinear optimization
Practical experience solving problems using large cloud-based datasets
Knowledge of scaling code with cloud computing resources such as AWS or GCP
